What does Rumi mean by "the reed's lament" in the Masnavi?
✦
In the Masnavi, Rumi opens with the ney (reed flute) as a central metaphor for the human soul. The reed cries because it was cut from the reed bed — just as the soul yearns to return to God, its divine origin.

The lament is not mere sadness; it is the music of longing. Rumi writes: "Listen to the reed, how it tells a tale, complaining of separations." This separation (firaq) is the engine of spiritual aspiration — it is what drives the seeker toward the Beloved.

What is the concept of fana in Sufism?
✦
Fanāʾ (فناء) means "annihilation" — the dissolution of the ego-self in the divine presence. It is one of the highest stations (maqāmāt) on the Sufi path.

Ibn Arabi and Al-Ghazali both describe fanāʾ not as destruction, but as a transcendence — the self is not erased, but its illusion of separateness from God is. What remains is baqāʾ (subsistence in God).

📚 Source: Ihya Ulum al-Din, Vol. IV; Fusus al-Hikam, Ch. 1
